Indian Wedding Traditions

Our wedding will incorporate several beautiful Hindu traditions. Here's what you can expect:

Baraat

The Baraat is the groom's wedding procession. Mickey will arrive at the wedding venue on a decorated vehicle or horse, accompanied by his family and friends who dance joyfully to the rhythm of dhol drums. This lively procession symbolizes the happiness of the groom's family in welcoming a new member to their family.

Mehndi

The Mehndi ceremony involves the application of intricate henna designs on the bride's hands and feet, as well as for female guests who wish to participate. These beautiful designs symbolize joy, beauty, and spiritual awakening. The deeper the color of the mehndi, the stronger the bond between the bride and groom is said to be.

Sangeet

The Sangeet is a pre-wedding celebration filled with music, dance, and festivities. Both families come together to perform choreographed dances, sing traditional songs, and celebrate the union of the couple. It's a time for relaxation and enjoyment before the more formal wedding ceremony.

We will have henna artists at the Sangeet who will be available to apply beautiful mehndi designs for all guests who are interested, regardless of gender or cultural background. This is a wonderful opportunity to participate in this beautiful tradition!

Hindu Ceremony

Our Hindu wedding ceremony will include several meaningful rituals:

  • Mandap: The ceremony takes place under a decorated canopy representing the home the couple will build together.
  • Kanyadaan: The giving away of the bride by her parents, symbolizing their blessing of the marriage.
  • Jai Mala: The exchange of flower garlands, representing the couple's acceptance of each other.
  • Mangal Phera: The couple walks around the sacred fire (Agni) four times, making vows of duty, prosperity, pleasure, and spirituality.
  • Saptapadi (Seven Steps): The couple takes seven steps together, each representing a marital vow.
  • Sindoor: The groom applies red powder (sindoor) to the bride's hairline, symbolizing her new status as a married woman.

Attire Guide

We welcome you to dress in a way that makes you feel comfortable and festive. Both Indian and Western attire are perfectly acceptable.

Indian Attire Options

For those interested in embracing Indian wedding fashion:

For Women:

  • Saree: A long, draped fabric wrapped around the body with a blouse. Rich fabrics like silk with embellishments are perfect for weddings.
  • Lehenga: A three-piece outfit consisting of a long skirt, a blouse (choli), and a scarf (dupatta). Especially popular for wedding celebrations.
  • Salwar Kameez/Anarkali: A long tunic (kameez) paired with loose pants (salwar) and a scarf (dupatta).

For Men:

  • Sherwani: A long coat-like garment paired with pants (usually churidar) for a formal look.
  • Kurta Pajama: A traditional long shirt paired with loose pants, offering a comfortable yet elegant option.
  • Nehru Jacket: A Western suit paired with a traditional Nehru collar jacket for a fusion look.

Western Attire Options

Western formal wear is completely acceptable for our celebration:

For Women:

  • Formal gowns or cocktail dresses
  • Elegant pantsuits or jumpsuits
  • Dressy separates in festive colors

For Men:

  • Suits or tuxedos
  • Dress shirts with slacks
  • Blazers with coordinating pants

Color Guidelines:

Indian weddings traditionally embrace vibrant colors. Feel free to wear bright, festive colors! The only color typically avoided by guests is solid red, as it's traditionally reserved for the bride.

Our wedding will be a colorful celebration, so don't be afraid to embrace vibrant hues and festive accessories. The most important thing is your comfort and enjoyment of our special day!
